A Simple Scenario

Imagine two colleagues trying to help the same struggling team member:

Sarah (Care-Dominant)
Care: 4.5, Fairness: 2.8
"They need immediate emotional support and compassion"
Marcus (Fairness-Dominant)
Fairness: 4.5, Care: 2.8
"They need to follow proper performance improvement procedures"

What happens? Sarah and Marcus clash. Each tries to force the other to see things "correctly." The struggling team member gets conflicting messages and feels worse. Both Sarah and Marcus feel frustrated and misunderstood.

This happens because they're stuck in OS1 mode (Operating System 1): forcing prediction agreement. Sarah needs Marcus to predict suffering. Marcus needs Sarah to predict procedural failure. Neither will coordinate until the other "sees it right."

There's Another Way

What if Sarah and Marcus could coordinate without agreeing on predictions?

OS2 mode (Operating System 2): They recognize their predictions are different, and that's okay. Instead of forcing agreement, they pool their capabilities:

  • Sarah monitors emotional wellbeing (her Care expertise)
  • Marcus designs fair improvement process (his Fairness expertise)
  • Together: compassionate AND fair support
  • Team member receives coordinated, complementary help

The key insight: Different predictions can coordinate through complementary capabilities.

The Validation Mechanism

Notice the pattern:

  • Each PRF discovers strategies from their phenomenological lens
  • Strategies are tested empirically (does λ improve?)
  • Successful strategies are added to pool
  • Convergence tests check if strategies work together
  • Pool grows through diverse testing, not theoretical agreement

Key insight: PRF diversity is not a problem to solve—it's the validation mechanism itself. The more perspectives that test the formalism, the faster the commons grows.
