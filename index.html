The Concept — Institutional Transmission

Every organization transmits its coordination culture across time. The people who thrive in the current system — who coordinate most effectively within its existing power structure — tend to become the templates for future hiring, promotion, and socialization. Those who cannot coordinate within the system tend to leave, be marginalized, or fall silent. Over generations, this selection pressure shapes who gets produced by the institution.

The UCP engine models this directly. After each simulated generation, the top-lambda agents — those with the highest coordination capacity in the current system — become the parents of the next cohort, passing their reasoning coefficients forward with some mutation. The question this app asks is: what kind of institution does that selection process build over time? Does coordination capacity improve? Does suppression grow or shrink? And what gets lost when only the well-coordinated are selected to reproduce the culture?

Top-λ Selection Fitness = Coordination Capacity Agents with highest λ Raw become parents of the next generation. The system selects for whoever coordinates best in the current structure — regardless of what they know that can't be safely said.
Random Selection No Selection Pressure All agents contribute equally to the next generation. A control condition — shows what transmission looks like without selection pressure toward high coordination.
PRF Transmission Inherited with Drift Reasoning coefficients (PRF kernels) pass from parents to children with small random mutations. The institution inherits its parents' moral frameworks — imperfectly, but recognizably.

📝 Discussion Prompts

  1. Did λ Raw improve across generations? If so, what does that mean in institutional terms — is a system that gets better at coordinating necessarily becoming more ethical?
  2. Compare the suppression gap in Generation 0 vs the final generation. Did selection pressure increase or decrease the gap? What does this tell you about what "fitness" means in this system?
  3. Read the agent voices in Generation 0 and the final generation side by side. Has the language changed? What seems to have been selected for — and what might have been selected out?
  4. Now run again with Random Selection as the comparison. How does removing selection pressure change the trajectory? What does this suggest about the role of institutional culture in producing the coordination outcomes you saw?
